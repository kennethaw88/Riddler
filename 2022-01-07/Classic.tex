\section*{Riddle:}

Amare the ant is traveling within Triangle ABC, as shown below.
Angle A measures 15 degrees, and sides AB and AC both have length 1.

\vspace{0.1in}
\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=4.5in]{diagram1.png}
\end{center}
\vspace{0.1in}

Amare starts at point B and wants to ultimately arrive on side AC.
However, the queen of his colony has asked him to make several stops along the way.
Specifically, his path must:

\begin{itemize}
\item Start at point B.
\item Second, touch a point---any point---on side AC.
\item Third, touch a point---any point---back on side AB.
\item Finally, proceed to a point---any point---on side AC (not necessarily the same point he touched earlier).
\end{itemize}

What is the shortest distance Amare can travel to complete the queen's desired path?

\section*{Solution:}

Traveling across the triangle three times can be reimagined as traveling across three stacked triangles.
I show this in the diagram below, in which $\triangle\mathrm{ABC}$, $\triangle\mathrm{ACD}$, and $\triangle\mathrm{ADE}$ are all similar triangles.

\vspace{0.1in}
\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=2.5in]{diagram2.png}
\end{center}
\vspace{0.1in}

In this case, the shortest total path length is simply a straight line that intersects $\overline{\mathrm{AE}}$ at a right angle.
If the intersection point is X, then $\angle\mathrm{AXB}$ is a right angle.
Because each of $\angle\mathrm{BAC}$, $\angle\mathrm{CAD}$, and $\angle\mathrm{DAE}$ are 15\textdegree, $\angle\mathrm{BAE}$ and $\angle\mathrm{BAX}$ are 45\textdegree.
Thus $\angle\mathrm{ABX}$ is also 45\textdegree, and $\triangle\mathrm{ABX}$ is an isosceles right triangle with a hypotenuse of 1.
So the two legs each have length $\nicefrac{\sqrt{2}}{2}$.
So the solution is \fcolorbox{red}{white}{$\bm{{\nicefrac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\approx0.707}}$}\,.
